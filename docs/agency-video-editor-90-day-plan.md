# 90-Day Plan to Reach $5,000/Month as a Remote Video Editor for Agencies

## Overview

This plan is designed for a working video editor who currently has a full-time corporate role, can dedicate about 5 to 15 hours per week to building a side business, prefers editing over strategy and sales, and wants AI to take as much work off the plate as possible. The target model is to become a remote plug-in editor for agencies serving e-commerce and online businesses, rather than selling directly to end clients.[cite:1][cite:2]

The most realistic path to $5,000 per month in this setup is not dozens of one-off jobs. It is a small number of recurring agency relationships that send regular overflow editing work, typically through monthly bundles or repeat project assignments.[cite:1][cite:3]

## Target Model

### Positioning

The service should be positioned as remote editing support for agencies that already manage strategy, clients, and creative direction. This matches the goal of staying focused on editing rather than heavy consulting or client hunting.[cite:2][cite:4]

A simple positioning statement:

- Remote video editor for agencies serving e-commerce and online businesses.
- Specializes in short-form ads, social edits, and longer brand/promotional videos.
- Reliable overflow support with clean communication and fast turnaround.

### Ideal Clients

The best-fit clients are:

- Small to midsize creative agencies
- Performance marketing agencies
- Social media agencies
- Content production agencies serving e-commerce brands

Agencies are a strong fit because they already have active client demand, often need overflow support, and commonly work with freelance editors on repeat assignments.[cite:2][cite:4]

## Revenue Math

The current starting rate is $300 for a 30 to 60 second edit with footage or direction provided and minimal graphics. That is a workable starter rate for agency test projects and smaller ad edits, especially while building recurring relationships.[cite:1]

Industry pricing references in 2026 commonly place freelance video editing around $35 to $75 per hour, with many 30 to 60 second branded or ad-style edits and recurring retainers priced well above entry-level rates depending on complexity and turnaround.[cite:1][cite:3]

A realistic path to $5,000/month could look like this:

| Client mix | Monthly amount | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Agency A | $1,200 | 4 short-form edits at $300 each |
| Agency B | $1,800 | 6 short-form edits at $300 each |
| Agency C | $2,000 | Mixed bundle of short-form edits plus 1 to 2 longer brand videos |
| Total | $5,000 | Small roster, recurring work |

Another workable version is 2 agencies at roughly $1,500 to $2,000 each plus one lighter overflow client around $1,000.[cite:1][cite:3]

## 90-Day Plan

## Month 1: Build the agency-ready foundation

### Goal

Create an agency-facing portfolio, clarify the offer, and start low-volume outreach with AI handling most of the research and writing.[cite:2][cite:5]

### Week 1: Reframe the portfolio

Actions:

- Update the website headline to clearly say the work is for agencies and online brands.
- Create a portfolio section labeled around agency and e-commerce editing work.
- Select 4 to 8 pieces that best show short-form promos, brand edits, or social-ready video work.
- Add one or two sentences under each example explaining where that type of edit fits, such as paid social, landing pages, or brand storytelling.

If the current portfolio lacks enough ad-style work, create 2 to 3 spec edits from existing footage, stock, or old projects. The goal is to make agencies instantly understand what kind of client work this editing supports.[cite:2][cite:5]

### Week 2: Build an AI-assisted lead list

Actions:

- Use AI tools to find 20 to 30 agencies that serve e-commerce or online brands.
- Focus on agencies that produce social content, paid ads, or short brand videos.
- Save agency name, website, email, LinkedIn, Instagram, and notes in a simple spreadsheet.

This list should prioritize agencies that look busy, have a visible portfolio, and are small enough that freelance overflow support is likely useful.[cite:2][cite:4]

### Week 3: Start outreach

Actions:

- Send 5 to 10 personalized outreach messages during the week.
- Use AI to draft each message after pasting in the agency site or about page.
- Keep each message short: compliment, fit, and a low-friction offer to help with overflow editing.

Cold outreach tends to work better when it is specific, short, and focused on the prospect rather than on a long self-introduction.[cite:5]

Sample structure:

- Mention one thing liked about their agency work.
- State the role clearly: remote editor for agencies handling e-commerce and online business content.
- Mention formats: short-form ads, promos, and brand edits.
- End with a simple CTA such as offering availability for overflow support.

### Week 4: Test project conversations

Actions:

- Reply quickly to any agency interest.
- Offer a simple paid test project using the $300 starting rate for short ad-style edits when appropriate.
- Track who replies, who opens conversations, and what type of agency seems most receptive.

The purpose of Month 1 is not necessarily to close a huge retainer immediately. It is to create traction, validate the positioning, and start conversations that can become recurring work.[cite:2][cite:4]

## Month 2: Turn interest into repeat work

### Goal

Land 1 to 2 agencies for test projects and convert at least one into recurring monthly work.[cite:2][cite:4]

### Week 5 to 6: Deliver smoothly

Actions:

- Use AI to convert client notes into checklists and edit action items.
- Deliver edits cleanly with simple communication and fast turnaround.
- Make the experience easy: clear file names, organized links, concise update messages.

Agencies often keep freelancers because they reduce internal friction, not only because of raw creative talent.[cite:4]

### Week 7 to 8: Propose bundles

Once one or two edits go well, propose a simple recurring structure.

Suggested starting bundles:

| Package | Monthly price | Scope |
|---|---:|---|
| Overflow Ad Pack | $1,200 | 4 short-form edits |
| Growth Ad Pack | $1,800 | 6 short-form edits |
| Mixed Brand Pack | $2,000 to $2,400 | 4 short-form edits plus 1 to 2 longer brand/promotional videos |

These prices are aligned with the stated $300 starting rate while leaving room to raise rates later as proof and relationships improve.[cite:1][cite:3]

At the same time, continue outreach every week so the pipeline does not depend on a single agency.

## Month 3: Stabilize income and move toward $5,000/month

### Goal

Build a small roster of 2 to 3 agencies and push from test-project mode into reliable monthly income.[cite:2][cite:4]

### Week 9 to 10: Focus on best-fit agencies

Actions:

- Double down on agencies that communicate clearly and send the kind of work that fits the preferred editing mix.
- Offer reserved monthly capacity for recurring clients.
- Continue adding 5 new agencies per week into the outreach pipeline.

With only 5 to 15 hours available weekly, the business should be built around a few high-fit relationships rather than broad volume.[cite:2]

### Week 11 to 12: Raise clarity, not complexity

Actions:

- Tighten the site and profiles further around agency editing support.
- Add one or two posts or case-style examples showing before/after edits or the types of work handled.
- Raise pricing slightly for new inquiries if demand is improving, especially for rush work or more complex edits.[cite:1][cite:3]

By the end of Month 3, success means having consistent monthly work, a repeatable outreach process, and enough demand to see a believable path to the full $5,000 target even if the exact number has not yet been hit.[cite:2][cite:4]

## Weekly Operating System

A simple weekly system keeps the plan realistic alongside a full-time job.

### Recommended weekly rhythm

| Task | Hours/week | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Outreach and follow-up | 1 to 3 | AI drafts messages; send 5 to 10 touches |
| Portfolio/profile improvement | 0.5 to 1 | Small updates only |
| Client work | 3 to 10 | Depends on project load |
| Admin and delivery | 0.5 to 1 | AI-assisted summaries and responses |

This makes the business manageable inside a 5 to 15 hour weekly limit while still generating momentum.[cite:2]

## AI Workflow

The operating principle is simple: AI does research, writing, summarizing, and first drafts; editing time is reserved for actual billable creative work.

### AI should handle:

- Finding agency prospects
- Summarizing agency fit
- Writing first drafts of emails and DMs
- Drafting proposals and service descriptions
- Turning briefs into checklists
- Drafting update emails and revision summaries

### Manual work should focus on:

- Final quality check on outreach
- Editing and delivery
- Relationship management with agencies that respond

This approach reduces the mental overhead of selling and admin while preserving the personal touch agencies still expect in outreach and communication.[cite:4][cite:5]

## Success Metrics

Progress should be measured weekly using simple leading indicators.

### Month 1 targets

- 20 to 30 agencies added to the list
- 5 to 10 outreach messages sent per week
- Portfolio repositioned for agencies and e-commerce work

### Month 2 targets

- 1 to 2 agency test projects
- At least 1 recurring monthly work conversation
- 1 simple package offer sent after a successful test project

### Month 3 targets

- 2 to 3 active agency relationships
- $1,500 to $3,000 in recurring work closed, with visible pipeline toward $5,000
- Repeatable AI-assisted outreach system running weekly

## Risks and how to handle them

### Risk 1: Not enough responses

If response rate is low after 3 to 4 weeks:

- Improve portfolio relevance, not just volume of outreach.
- Narrow agency targeting more tightly to e-commerce and social-focused shops.
- Shorten outreach messages further and make them more specific.[cite:5]

### Risk 2: Underpricing

A $300 starting rate is fine for cleaner, simpler edits, but it should not remain the ceiling once trust is established. Increase pricing for more revisions, faster turnaround, more complex graphics, or stronger ongoing demand.[cite:1][cite:3]

### Risk 3: Time overload

With a full-time job, accepting too many custom one-off projects can create stress quickly. Favor repeatable agency work with similar formats and clearer systems.[cite:2]

## Recommended next moves

The best immediate next steps are:

1. Reframe the website and Vimeo around agency support.
2. Build the first 20 to 30 agency lead list with AI.
3. Send the first 5 to 10 AI-assisted outreach messages.
4. Prepare one simple one-sheet or email-ready description of the offer.
5. Treat the first agency project as a bridge to monthly recurring work, not just a one-time job.

## Bottom line

The most realistic route to $5,000/month in this situation is to become a dependable remote editor for 2 to 3 agencies that serve e-commerce and online businesses. With a clear offer, agency-facing portfolio, starter pricing around $300 for simpler edits, and AI doing most of the prospecting and writing work, that goal is achievable through recurring relationships rather than marketplace platforms or constant direct-to-brand selling.[cite:1][cite:2][cite:3][cite:4][cite:5]
